<p><p>The amphibious vehicle market features various propulsion types tailored for versatile environments. Screw propeller propulsion utilizes rotating blades to move through water, offering efficiency for various water conditions. Water jet propulsion relies on high-pressure water streams for thrust, providing agility and a smooth ride, ideal for shallow waters. Track-based propulsion employs continuous tracks for traversing land and soft terrains, enhancing stability and mobility. Each type serves specific operational needs, catering to military, rescue, and recreational applications.</p></p>
