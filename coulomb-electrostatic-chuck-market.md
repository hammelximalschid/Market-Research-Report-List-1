<p><p>Coulomb Electrostatic Chucks (ESCs) are classified into unipolar and multipolar types based on their electrode configurations. Unipolar ESCs utilize a single electrode configuration, generating a uniform electric field, ideal for simpler applications requiring straightforward clamping. In contrast, multipolar ESCs employ multiple electrodes, allowing for more complex electric field distributions, which enhance performance in intricate processes, such as semiconductor manufacturing. Each type caters to specific industry needs, balancing efficiency and precision for diverse applications.</p></p>
