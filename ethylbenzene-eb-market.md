<p><p>Ethylbenzene (EB) is primarily produced through three main methods: the AlCl3 liquid hydrocarbon method, the zeolite gas catalytic method, and the zeolite liquid catalytic method. The AlCl3 method involves aluminum chloride as a catalyst for the alkylation of benzene with ethylene. In contrast, the zeolite gas catalytic method uses zeolite as a catalyst in vapor phase reactions, while the zeolite liquid catalytic method employs liquid-phase reactions for greater efficiency. Each method impacts production cost, yield, and environmental considerations.</p></p>
