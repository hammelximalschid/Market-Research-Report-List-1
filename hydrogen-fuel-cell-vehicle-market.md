<p><p>Hydrogen Fuel Cell Vehicles (FCVs) utilize different fuel cell technologies to convert hydrogen into electricity. **Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFCs)** are the most common, offering high efficiency, quick startup, and compact design, ideal for passenger vehicles. **Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells (PAFCs)** operate at higher temperatures, providing better heat utilization but are bulkier, often used in stationary applications. **Other fuel cell types**, like Solid Oxide or Alkaline Fuel Cells, cater to niche markets, offering unique advantages such as high-temperature operation or cost-effectiveness for specific industrial or heavy-duty applications.</p></p>
