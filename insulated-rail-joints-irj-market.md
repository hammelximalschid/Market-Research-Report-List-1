<p><p>Insulated Rail Joints (IRJs) are critical components in railway systems, designed to electrically isolate sections of track for signaling and control purposes. The market consists primarily of two types: Supported IRJs and Suspended IRJs. Supported IRJs are designed to rest directly on the ties or sleepers, ensuring stability and resistance to stress. Conversely, Suspended IRJs are elevated above the track bed, allowing for greater thermal expansion and movement, which is vital in varying temperature conditions, enhancing rail functionality and safety.</p></p>
