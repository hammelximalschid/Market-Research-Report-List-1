<p><p>Laminar flow hoods are essential for maintaining a sterile environment in laboratories, categorized primarily into horizontal and vertical flow types. Horizontal laminar flow hoods direct filtered air from the back to the front, providing an unobstructed workspace ideal for tasks requiring wide access. Vertical flow hoods, on the other hand, draw air from the top, creating a downward airflow that minimizes contamination, making them suitable for working with larger equipment or substances. Both types ensure safety and cleanliness in scientific applications.</p></p>
