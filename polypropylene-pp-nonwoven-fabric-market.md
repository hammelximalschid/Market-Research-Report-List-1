<p><p>Polypropylene (PP) nonwoven fabric is categorized into three main types: meltblown, spunbonded, and staple fibers. Meltblown fabrics consist of fine fibers produced by extruding molten polypropylene, resulting in materials that are lightweight and highly absorbent, often used in filters and medical applications. Spunbonded fabrics are created by bonding continuous filaments, offering strength and durability, commonly used in packaging and construction. Staple fiber nonwovens are made from short fibers, providing versatility for various applications, including hygiene products and automotive interiors.</p></p>
