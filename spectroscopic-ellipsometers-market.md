<p><p>Spectroscopic ellipsometers are categorized based on their operating wavelength ranges: UV (ultraviolet), VIS (visible), NIR (near-infrared), and IR (infrared). The UV market focuses on thin film and semiconductor characterizations, leveraging short wavelengths for high sensitivity. The VIS market targets applications in materials science and biology, providing insights into surface properties. NIR ellipsometers detect organic and chemical compounds, while IR systems are used for studying larger structures and bulk materials. Each type serves distinct scientific and industrial needs.</p></p>
