<p><p>The Swept Frequency Capacitive Sensing (SFCS) market encompasses various technologies based on capacitive sensing principles. Surface Capacitance measures changes in capacitance at the surface level, ideal for touch sensors. Projected Capacitance extends sensing capabilities by using electric fields to detect touch at a distance. Self-Capacitance involves measuring individual capacitors, allowing for more precise detection, while Mutual Capacitance uses capacitance changes between pairs of electrodes, enabling multi-touch and large-area sensing, enhancing user interface experiences in electronic devices.</p></p>
