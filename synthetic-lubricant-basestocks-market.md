<p><p>Synthetic lubricant basestocks are categorized into several types based on their chemical structure and properties. Polyalkylene glycols (PAGs) offer excellent thermal stability and low volatility. Polyalphaolefins (PAOs) are popular for their high-performance capabilities, including low-temperature fluidity. Polyisobutenes provide excellent sealing and viscosity properties. Phosphate esters are known for their anti-wear and extreme-pressure performance, while synthetic esters offer biodegradable options with superior lubrication. Other categories may include various specialized synthetic basestocks tailored for specific applications.</p></p>
