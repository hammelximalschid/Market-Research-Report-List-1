<p><p>The waste fill-level sensor market is primarily segmented into two types: ultrasonic and laser sensors. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to detect the distance to the waste surface, providing accurate fill-level measurements. They are cost-effective and suitable for various waste types. In contrast, laser sensors employ light beams to measure fill levels, offering higher precision and longer detection ranges. These sensors are ideal for challenging environments but come at a higher cost. Both technologies enhance waste management efficiency and monitoring.</p></p>
